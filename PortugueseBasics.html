Greetings and Common Phrases (Frases Comúnes)

When it comes to learning Portuguese, there are simply some words and phrases you have to know.

  • Tudo bem? - 'What's up/Everything good?'
  • Bom dia - 'Good morning'
  • Boa tarde - 'Good afternoon'
  • Boa noite - 'Good night'
  • Até logo - 'Until later'

  • Não obrigado - 'No thank you'
  • Às vezes - 'Sometimes'
  • Nunca - 'Never'

Common Portuguese Verbs (Verbos Comuns em Português)

  • Falar - 'to speak'
  • Comer - 'to eat'
  • Beber - 'to drink'
  • Fazer - 'to do/to make'
  • Caminhar - 'to walk'
  • Ver - 'to see/watch'
  • Ser - 'to be'
  • Estar - 'to be'
  • Ter - 'to have'
  • Comprar - 'to buy'
  • Dormir - 'to sleep'
  • Trabalhar - 'to work'

Notice how both 'Ser' and 'Estar' mean 'to be', however, they are used in different contexts. Typically speaking, Ser is used to describe things which are permanent and Estar is used to describe temporary things.

Examples: 'Eu sou alto.' / 'Eu estou muito cansado.' - Being tall is a permanent feature, while being sleepy is typically not permament.

Colors (Colores)

  • Vermelho - 'Red'
  • Laranja - 'Orange'
  • Amarelo - 'Yellow'
  • Verde - 'Green'
  • Azul - 'Blue'
  • Roxo/a - 'Purple'
  • Preto/a - 'Black'
  • Branco/a - 'White'
  • Cinza - 'Gray'
  • Marróm - 'Brown'
  • Claro/a - 'Light'
  • Escuro/a - 'Dark'

Note that while some of these colors end in either -a or -o, if they are describing a subject which is masculine or feminine, they may change their endings. For example, the Portuguese word for 'white' is Branco, but if you are talking about a house, it would be: 'A casa branca'.
