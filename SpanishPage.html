SPANISH

Circa 13th Century

Español, even the name sounds sexy and instantly gives visuals of flamenco guitar chords being strummed in ancient cobble-stone streets. With nearly 500 million native speakers, Spanish is recognized as the second-most spoken native language in the world! With over 20 countries around the world on three continents recognizing it as an official language, who wouldn't want to learn Spanish!?

Not sure how to get started? Don't worry, Language Buddy gives you a plethora of resources to help you get started with your new buddy! Make sure to check out our valuable Resources and Tips page to get you started speaking a new language today!

HOW SPANISH SOUNDS

The Spanish language is known for its reverberating "R's" and its palate-tasting "Ñ's". Don't feel intimidated though, Spanish actually shares many of the same sounds as English, so you can learn with relative ease. Spanish is also a phonetic language, meaning that words are pronounced as they are spelled, making it easy to follow when written. Also, don't be intimidated if you can't pronounce the "rr". Many native Spanish speakers struggle with this sound as well and Language Buddy has many great resources to help you master this sound! Soon enough, you'll be saying words like "Rico" and "Arriba" like you hear in the movies!

Accents and Regions


Given that Spanish is spoken all around the world by hundreds of millions of people, it's natural that many accents and dialects have evolved over the years. Within Mexico for example, the Spanish spoken in Southern, tropical Oaxaca sounds different to the accent found in the Northern deserts of Juárez. This principle applies to most Spanish-speaking countries, where not only do accents change based on country, but on what region you are in. This means that, for example, some of the words used widely in Puerto Rican Spanish might not make sense to someone from Southern Spain. Don't worry though, Language Buddy has users from all over the Spanish-speaking world, who want to teach you this beautiful language and all is has to offer!
