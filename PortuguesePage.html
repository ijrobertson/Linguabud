PORTUGUESE

Circa 1143 AD

Portuguese. From refreshing caipirinhas on the beach to lush amazonian rainforests, Portuguese has roots in all things beautiful...and that's just in Brazil. Spoken by aproximately 270 million people worldwide, Portuguese is quickly becoming a popular language amongst linguists and polyglots alike. Portuguese is listed as an official langauge in 10 countries spanning 4 continents, making it a truly global and truly important language.

Not sure how to get started? Don't worry, Language Buddy gives you a plethora of resources to help you get started with your new buddy! Make sure to check out our valuable Resources and Tips page to get you started speaking a new language today!

HOW PORTUGUESE SOUNDS

Circa 1143 AD

Portuguese is often described as a 'nasal' language. There's a lot going on in the nose. Don't let that fool you, however, because many say that Portuguese sounds like a relaxed Spanish. That being said, Portuguese does have sounds that are found in neither English, nor Spanish, like the "ão" found in 'não'. To make this sound, try saying the English word 'now', but hum it.

Check out this chart to the left to learn some new Portuguese words and see how they are pronounced!

Accents and Regions


Just as you will find in most languages, Portuguese has many different accents and words used depending on the country you are in. In Portugal and Rio de Janeiro for example, people pronounce the 's' when found at the end of a word as "sh". As in "Português". For English speakers, think about the difference between the Brazilian accent and the Portuguese accent, as unique as the difference between the American accent and the British accent.
