ITALIAN

Circa 14th Century

Continuously ranked as one of the world's sexiest languages, Italian has the power to steal hearts. Italian culture reflects a deep passion for all things enjoyable in life, making notable recognition with it's: food, art, literature, and music, to name a few. With somewhere between 60-80 million speakers around the world, it's also a practical language to learn! Beyond that, Italian is very closely related to Latin, thus, making it an easy transition to and from any other Romance Language.

Not sure how to get started? Don't worry, Language Buddy gives you a plethora of resources to help you get started with your new buddy! Make sure to check out our valuable Resources and Tips page to get you started speaking a new language today!

HOW ITALIAN SOUNDS

Known for its smoothness and its expressiveness; people often say that Italian speakers sound like they are singing. You don't just speak with your mouth, but also your hands! Whether it is the rolling of the "r" or the "tz" with the letter "z", Italian mesmerizes anyone willing to listen.

Accents and Regions


Even though Italian is primarily only spoken in Italy, that doesn't mean that all Italian is the same. The language boasts a wide range of accents and dialects, making it a uniquely diverse language. Even when traveling to neighboring villages and cities, native Italians can have a difficult time understading their fellow Italians.
